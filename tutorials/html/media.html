Media: Audio, Video & Embeds Intermediate

Video is most of the internet's traffic. HTML gives you native players with three lines — the depth is in formats, captions (a legal requirement), autoplay policy, and the streaming reality behind every production video product.

Video — the Production Element

<video controls width="640" height="360"
       poster="/img/intro-poster.jpg" preload="metadata">
  <source src="/media/intro.webm" type="video/webm">
  <source src="/media/intro.mp4"  type="video/mp4">
  <track kind="captions" src="/media/intro.en.vtt" srclang="en" label="English" default>
  <track kind="captions" src="/media/intro.hi.vtt" srclang="hi" label="हिन्दी">
  Your browser doesn't support video — <a href="/media/intro.mp4">download it</a>.
</video>
AttributeDeep detail
controlsNative UI: play, seek, volume, fullscreen, PiP. Omit only when building custom controls via the media JS API (play(), currentTime, events)
posterFrame shown before play. Without it: first frame or black box. Choose a poster that sells the click.
preloadnone (nothing until play — galleries of many videos), metadata (duration/dimensions only — the usual right answer), auto (browser may buffer everything — costly on mobile)
<source> orderFirst supported type wins — put the best-compressed first (WebM/AV1 before MP4/H.264 fallback)
width/heightSame CLS story as images: reserve the space

Autoplay Policy — Why Your Video Won't Start

<video autoplay muted loop playsinline src="/media/hero-bg.mp4"></video>

Browsers block autoplay with audio (after years of user rage). The only reliable autoplay is muted; iOS additionally needs playsinline or it hijacks to fullscreen. JS video.play() returns a Promise — it rejects when blocked; production players catch it and show a tap-to-play state. Every silent hero-background video you've seen uses exactly the four attributes above.

Audio

<audio controls preload="none" src="/media/podcast-ep42.mp3"></audio>

Same API as video minus the picture. Podcast pattern: preload="none" on episode lists (20 players ≠ 20 downloads), plus MediaSession API in JS for lock-screen controls.

Iframes — Embedding Other Pages Safely

<iframe src="https://www.youtube-nocookie.com/embed/VIDEO_ID"
        title="Deployment course intro"          ⟵ REQUIRED for a11y
        loading="lazy"                            ⟵ below-the-fold embeds
        allowfullscreen
        sandbox="allow-scripts allow-same-origin allow-presentation"
        referrerpolicy="strict-origin-when-cross-origin"></iframe>
AttributeWhy
titleWhat screen readers announce for the frame — "iframe" alone is useless
sandboxDefault-deny: no scripts, no forms, no popups — re-allow only what's needed. (This site's Playground output runs in sandbox="allow-scripts" — that's why arbitrary code is safe there.)
allowPermissions Policy per-frame: allow="camera; microphone" for embedded meeting widgets
Performance noteEach iframe is a full nested page. YouTube's embed costs ~1MB — the "lite embed" pattern (thumbnail + click loads the real iframe) is standard on fast sites.

How Real Video Actually Ships (the streaming picture)

Your MP4 on a server  →  fine for a 30s demo
Production (YouTube/Netflix/Hotstar):
  original → transcoded to ladder: 240p / 480p / 720p / 1080p / 4K
           → chopped into 2–6s segments (HLS .m3u8 / DASH .mpd)
           → player measures bandwidth per segment, switches quality seamlessly
  <video> element stays — hls.js/dash.js feed it via Media Source Extensions

That's "adaptive bitrate streaming" — the reason playback survives your train going through a tunnel. The full architecture (storage, CDN, DRM) lives in the System Design track.

Interview Questions

Q1: Why does video.play() sometimes throw, and how do products handle it?

Autoplay policy: play() with audible sound outside a user gesture rejects the promise. Products catch the rejection and fall back to muted autoplay or a visible play button. Never assume playback started — await the promise.

Q2: captions vs subtitles?

Captions: for viewers who can't hear — dialogue plus meaningful sounds ("[door slams]"), same language. Subtitles: for viewers who can't understand the language — translated dialogue only. Different audiences, different legal weight (captions are the compliance requirement).

Q3: What does iframe sandbox actually do?

Applies a default-deny policy to the framed document: scripts, form submission, popups, top-level navigation, plugin content all blocked until individually re-allowed via tokens. Critical rule: never combine allow-scripts + allow-same-origin for untrusted same-origin content — together they let the frame remove its own sandbox.

🏋️ Practice:
1. In the Playground, build a video player with poster, two sources, and a captions track (write 3 cues of WebVTT by hand — you can use a data: URL for the track src).
2. Make an autoplaying muted looping background video and confirm it plays; remove muted and watch it get blocked (check the console).
3. Embed any YouTube video with title, lazy loading, and the nocookie domain.

🎯 Key Takeaways

  • Multiple sources best-first; poster + preload="metadata" as the sane defaults.
  • Captions are law and retention, not garnish; WebVTT + kind="captions".
  • Autoplay = muted (+playsinline on iOS); play() returns a promise that can reject.
  • Iframes need title + sandbox + lazy; they're full pages with full costs.
  • <video> is the front end of a streaming pipeline — segments and bitrate ladders do the real work.
